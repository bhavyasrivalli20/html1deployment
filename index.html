    Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)

    Definition

    SDLC is a process used by the software industry to design, develop, and test high-quality software that meets customer expectations.

    Objectives

    1. Produce high-quality software that meets customer expectations.
    2. Ensure a structured and disciplined approach to software development.

    Characteristics

    1. SDLC is a methodology that defines the entire procedure of software development step-by-step.
    2. It's a structured process used to design, develop, and test good-quality software.

    Importance

    1. Without SDLC, software development would not be systematic or disciplined.
    2. SDLC ensures clear understanding among team members about what to do and when to do it.
    3. It helps managers monitor project progress effectively.

    SDLC Models

    Waterfall Model

    The Waterfall model is a linear, sequential project management approach where each phase of development must be completed before moving on to the next, similar to how water flows down a waterfall; it's considered a structured method with distinct stages like requirement analysis, design, implementation, testing, and deployment, where each phase builds upon the previous one without backtracking, making it ideal for projects with clearly defined requirements and minimal flexibility.

    Agile Model

    Agile methodology is a project management approach that emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and continuous improvement by breaking projects into smaller, iterative phases called "sprints," allowing teams to quickly adapt to changing needs and deliver working software in increments, prioritizing customer feedback throughout the development process.

    Types

    Important Types of Agile Methodologies:

    1.Kanban.
    2.Scrum.
    3.Extreme Programming (XP)
    4.Feature-Driven Development (FDD)

    What is kanban?

    Kanban is a popular framework used to implement Agile and DevOps software development. It requires real-time communication of capacity and full transparency of work. Work items are represented visually on a kanban board, allowing team members to see the state of every piece of work at any time.

    What is Scrum?

    Scrum is a popular Agile framework used for managing and completing complex projects. It emphasizes teamwork, accountability, and iterative progress toward well-defined goals.

    .

    Key Components

    1. Product Owner: Responsible for defining and prioritizing the product backlog.
    2. Scrum Master: Facilitates the Scrum process, removes impediments, and ensures the team follows Scrum principles.
    3. Development Team: A cross-functional team of developers, testers, and designers who work together to develop the product.
    4. Sprint: A time-boxed iteration (usually 2-4 weeks) where the team works on a specific set of tasks from the product backlog.
    5. Daily Scrum: A 15-minute meeting where team members share their progress, plans, and any obstacles.
